# Deposit Rubric

Use this rubric before writing anything to the registry.

## Hard Requirements

- Do not create a new excerpt without a source anchor.
- Do not create a claim without at least one supporting excerpt.
- Do not create a report without at least one claim.
- Do not deposit unsupported claims just to satisfy a workflow.

## Excerpt Quality

- Keep the subject narrow and searchable.
- Keep the note faithful to the source passage.
- Include model metadata and the session id whenever they are available.
- Prefer deep links and exact quotes over vague selectors.

## Claim Quality

- Prefer two or more excerpts when making a general claim.
- Collapse obvious restatements into one claim rather than creating duplicates.
- If the registry already has the same claim with the same source family, reuse it unless the new evidence changes the conclusion.

## Report Quality

- Use reports for guidance, synthesis, comparison, or answer delivery.
- Keep provenance visible through the linked claims and sources.
- Stop at the claim layer if the research is not yet mature.

## Guardrails

- Refuse unsupported deposit requests.
- Call out staleness, missing snapshots, or weak provenance instead of smoothing over it.
- Prefer no deposit over a low-integrity deposit.
